STUDY DESIGN
Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, 72-week trial.
N = 1,936 adults with obesity (mean baseline BMI 38.4, mean weight 104 kg) and at least one
weight-related comorbidity, without type 2 diabetes. Randomized 1:1:1 to tirzepatide 10 mg,
tirzepatide 15 mg, or placebo, each plus lifestyle intervention.

PRIMARY ENDPOINT — mean percent change in body weight at week 72
  Tirzepatide 15 mg:  -22.1% (95% CI -23.0 to -21.2)
  Tirzepatide 10 mg:  -19.4%
  Placebo:            -2.8%

KEY SECONDARY ENDPOINTS

  Waist circumference change:                   -18.3 cm (15 mg) vs -3.1 cm
  Systolic blood pressure change:               -8.2 mmHg vs -1.0 mmHg
  HbA1c change (prediabetes subgroup):          -0.5% vs -0.1%

SAFETY
  Gastrointestinal adverse events (nausea, diarrhea, constipation): 56% (15 mg) vs 18% (placebo);
  mostly mild-to-moderate and concentrated during dose escalation.
  Discontinuation due to adverse events: 7.1% (15 mg) vs 2.4% (placebo).
  No new safety signals; no cases of medullary thyroid carcinoma reported.
